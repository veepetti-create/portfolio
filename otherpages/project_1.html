University News Redesign

October-November 2024, May 2025

This design is an updated version of the one I originally submitted for class, as I knew there was room for improvement. I adjusted the spacing, refined the page content, and repositioned images to create a more visually organized layout. In earlier iterations, the larger gaps between sections made the page feel somewhat disconnected, so I focused on tightening the layout to achieve better visual flow. This project made me think more critically about how I approach website design and reminded me of the saying “less is more.” For much of the process, I was trying to include more content than was necessary, but since my professor encouraged us to modify the content freely, I took that opportunity to simplify and focus on what mattered most.



Sketches

My focus during the sketching phase was to ensure that information could be easily viewed and that each section was understandable without relying on heavy visual separation. Since there was a large amount of content to include, I used the sketches to explore how much information should be represented and how to balance clarity with layout density.



Iteration

When I first began working on my designs, I experimented with different groupings and text arrangements. In these early iterations, the spacing lacked consistency, and looking back, the second iteration in particular felt cluttered and less visually balanced.
